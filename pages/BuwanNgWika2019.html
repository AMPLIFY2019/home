APEC Schools- Taytay Strikes for Buwan ng Wika 2019

Askhiea Ashley Amistoso Oct 17, 2019 Article

As recognition to our national language, APEC Schools- Taytay celebrated the Buwan ng Wika with the theme “Wikang Katutubo: Tungo sa Isang Bansang Filipino” that aims for the nation to be instilled with an utmost dedication of value and role of the Filipino language and the indigenous languages in building a nation that understands each other throughout the country last August 31, 2019 at the building’s third floor..

The celebration was divided into 2 parts for the AM and PM shifts last August 31, 2019 at the campus’ third floor. It is spearheaded by the chosen emcees from different sections to introduce and entertain the students throughout the program.

It is a month-long preparation as all of the students from every section are encouraged to perform with the different categories connected to every place that were assigned to them. It includes the designs for their bulletin boards, doors, pagbasa at pagsulat ng baybayin, pagsulat ng pabula, paggawa ng patalastas, paggawa ng poster/slogan, and the most awaited “Lakan and Lakambini” competition.

Different sections competed through showcasing the place and festival that was assigned to them. Every decoration that was used are mostly from handmade and recycled materials. Everyone was encouraged to witness the overall view of the program as part of the celebration.

The Lakan and Lakambini is one of the highlights of the event as every representative of each section showcased their traditional costumes that were made from pure recycled materials. 11AM-1 won both title for the AM shift while the 11PM-1 won the Lakan and 12PM-1 for the Lakambini for the PM shift.

“It is really fun. I’ve already experienced it last school year, it’s the same feeling as what I had before. It is not just the program that I really like but it also gives me the feeling of how important our pambansang wika is. This is not just a celebration but it symbolize us being a Filipino citizen.“ , stated by Mary Grace Moreno, a 12PM-1 student.

As a concluding activity, all students from every section had their own gathering and celebration inside their respective classrooms with their own version of feast.
